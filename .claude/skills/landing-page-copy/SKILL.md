# Landing Page Copy

**The premise:** the difference between landing pages that earn and pages that don't is just
well-written text - something anyone can change, whether or not they can code.

**What a landing page is:** the website of a campaign - one page that acts as the "collection
point" for the whole campaign. Every ad for the campaign points to it.

**One goal:** a landing page must have exactly 1 goal. Build the whole page around it.

**Tools for building one without coding:** any simple website builder or an emailing platform
with a page builder works (e.g. a hosted website builder, or an email tool like MailerLite).
Pick whatever the user already uses.

**Language:** produce output in the brand's language (default Slovak), in a plain, direct,
no-bullshit voice.

## 8 tips to (re)write a landing page

1. **Anger the visitor - find them an enemy.** Provoke frustration with how things are done
   now. If you anger them, they start looking for a solution - which you then offer. How:
   identify a market problem that angers you and your audience, evoke the "pain" of that
   problem with emotional copy (STOP), then present your solution below.
2. **Focus on your USP.** Identify ONE USP and stick to it across the whole page. The USP is
   what you do differently, the thing that makes a client think "Finally! Someone who
   offers...". Because of the USP the visitor remembers you and compares you to competitors.
3. **Prove you're telling the truth.** If the page says you're the best/biggest, prove it:
   client reviews, client logos, Google ratings, awards/certifications, exact numbers (no
   abstractions), photos of the product in use, product videos, diagrams, charts, a demo to
   try if possible.
4. **Focus on benefits - the "what".** Benefits are what the client can achieve by using your
   product. Use tip 3 to prove the benefits are real. Prove what you claim a person achieves
   with your product or service.
5. **Simplify the text.** Go through it 3 times on 3 different days and each time cut or
   simplify something. Don't use trendy sales words and formulas - say it like a human. Trick:
   say it the way you'd explain your work to a new person at a bar. Use scannable text.
6. **Address doubts.** Visitors will have doubts about your product. The best way to resolve
   them is to say them out loud - write them on the page. Identify doubts by asking your actual
   clients. Common doubts: How much does it cost? Do I have to enter a credit card? Is there a
   guarantee? How long does setup take? What's different here vs competitors? How can I trust
   you? And the most important: "What the hell is this?!"
7. **Use images that say the same thing as the text.** Seeing once beats reading 100 times;
   seeing AND reading is gold. Avoid generic illustrations and "happy people" stock images.
8. **Collect contacts before launching the campaign.** This is the technique of warming up
   contacts before a campaign (useful before high-pressure sales seasons like Black Friday and
   Christmas).

## Summary checklist (what a landing page needs before launch)
- Anger someone who arrives. Immediately. Through emotion.
- Identify 1 USP and stick to it across the whole page.
- Prove that the text on the page is true.
- Describe benefits: what can you achieve if you buy my...?
- Simplify the text 3 times, written for scanning.
- Address doubts before they even arise.
- Use images that say the same thing as the text.
- Collect contacts. If a person doesn't buy, give them something free in exchange for an email.

## How to use this skill
1. Confirm the page's single goal first; structure everything around it.
2. Draft top-to-bottom in this order: enemy/frustration -> solution -> USP -> proof ->
   benefits -> objections -> CTA, with supporting images.
3. Run the simplify-3-times pass and the "explain it at a bar" test on the final copy.
4. Add a contact-capture offer for non-buyers.
