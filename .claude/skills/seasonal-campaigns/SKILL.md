# Seasonal Campaigns (Black Friday / Q4)

**The frame:** Q4 is the marketing holiday every year. Pressure rises from early November and
detonates with Black Friday, then Cyber Monday. Brands pour hundreds of thousands of euros a
day into ads, so ad and click prices hit their yearly maximum. The two strategies below let a
small or large player avoid getting lost in that noise. Both can be set up cheaply, by anyone,
e.g. through Facebook.

**Language:** produce output in the brand's language (default Slovak), in a plain, direct,
no-bullshit voice.

## The cold-to-hot contact journey
A person goes from cold (first contact with your brand) to hot (ready to buy). In November,
when everyone is bidding ad prices up, you can easily get lost UNLESS you already have hot
contacts - the people who think of you when they buy. Start building them today, via email or
a Facebook event. Know your contact: is it warm or cold? Map it on the customer-journey
diagram.

## Strategy 1: Warm up your customers (BEFORE Black Friday)
- **Start collecting emails.** Social media won't help here - for the next 2 months it is pure
  noise and the ad/click price hits its yearly max. Ask for emails and give something valuable
  in exchange (part of a course or book free, a contest).
- **Tooling:** an email tool with a free landing-page builder lets you stand up a
  contact-collection page in a day (e.g. Mailchimp or any equivalent).
- **Prepare to send newsletters.** Your email contacts will already know you; warm them with
  welcome messages so they know exactly who is writing and why.
- All of this is still doable before Black Friday.

## Strategy 2: Turn off (cold) ads - use remarketing (DURING Black Friday)
- Ads are expensive now. Convincing someone who has never seen your product, during such
  pressure, is very expensive. Speak to people who are already fans instead.
- **Turn on remarketing** to people who follow you or have already visited your website.
- **Recommendation:** if your product is an impulse buy (under ~10 EUR), keep a classic Black
  Friday campaign running to people who don't know you on ~20% of budget - but watch its
  performance and whether it pays off.
- **How:** run it through Facebook Ads Manager. You don't need a Facebook Pixel or other site
  tools - in step 2 of ad creation choose "create new custom audience". You can even remarket
  to people who often save your Instagram posts. Explore the options and pick what fits.

## Prepare the creative
- **Consistency:** use the same images, texts, and colors of the campaign across every medium
  - Facebook, Instagram, your website, the email newsletter. This holds for all campaigns, not
  just Black Friday.
- **Social proof:** show how your product is used and how many / what kind of people use it.
  Seeing real people buy and use it makes a prospect more likely to buy too. Re-shared stories
  from satisfied customers work very well for this.
- **FOMO (fear of missing out):** add a time limit, a countdown in the email, and limited
  capacity. "Only 1 spot left in the course" / "1 hour left of this sale" still work. Really.
- **A small bonus closes the deal:** a free set of small items, free shipping, etc. These
  bonuses are often what tips an already-warmed customer into buying.
- **Test ugly creatives:** make several graphics and test even the truly ugly variants. For
  ~99% of clients the ugliest ones sell the most.

## Tactic: time it right (before, during, AND after Black Friday)
- Start collecting customers - by email, a Facebook event, or even just by collecting reach:
  post 1 post every day in November.
- Until ~25 November, run a solid reach campaign on the profile. Use this pre-Black-Friday
  pressure window to collect cold contacts.
- Regular posts turn a profile visitor into a fan, which warms them up via this quick tactic.
- Black Friday arrives: with reach collected or contacts warmed, focus on conversion via
  remarketing. Sell to people who visited your profile in recent days/months/year and already
  know you. Targeting people who don't know you would be too expensive now.
- **Beginner recommendation:** learn to run Instagram/Facebook ads via Facebook Ads Manager;
  the ad-manager basics take about half an hour to learn.
- **Stretch Black Friday to a week or two** as needed, but remember Cyber Monday - the Monday
  after, historically very strong for purchases. Use remarketing again, but exclude people who
  already bought from you.

## How to use this skill
1. Place the user on the timeline: before / during / after Black Friday.
2. Before: stand up email collection (lead magnet) + warm-up sequence; run reach to gather cold
   contacts.
3. During: switch to remarketing on warm audiences; keep cold ads only for sub-10-EUR impulse
   buys on ~20% budget.
4. Layer the creative tactics: consistency, social proof, FOMO/countdown, bonus, ugly-variant
   test.
5. Don't forget Cyber Monday and excluding existing buyers from remarketing.
