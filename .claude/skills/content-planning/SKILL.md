# Content Planning & Production Workflow

**Structure of the system:** Part A (steps 1-3) = WHAT to create and how to get ideas. Part B
(steps 4-7) = the PROCESS: how to set up creation and when to do what.

**Language:** produce output in the brand's language (default Slovak), in a plain, direct,
no-bullshit voice.

## 1/7 What posts to create - know your audience first
If you know someone, it's easier to talk to them. Same with content. Most creators don't know
their audience, don't know what problems it has, then wonder why reach is small. Knowing the
audience has just 2 steps:

1. **Name your 3 best clients.** Ideally ones you know personally and know are your
   ultra-clients. First and last name. If you have no clients, write who you WANT as a client
   (a specific real person you know).
2. **Describe each one.** Age, occupation, interests, what problems they solve, why they work
   with you. Example: "Client 1: a 20-year-old marketing student, huge drive to work but
   doesn't know where or how to start."

Then feed that audience description to an AI assistant (you can do this right here) and ask it
for post ideas. Iterate: tell it which ideas you liked and which you didn't, so it learns how
you think. Aim for **10-20 viable topics** you can talk about.

## 2/7 How to generate post ideas - hunt insights ("AHA!" moments)
The best posts make the audience think "AHA! That's so me." That's the work of a great insight.
To find them, reuse the SAME thread from step 1 (it already knows your audience and its
problems), then ask this formula:

> Ake aha! Momenty alebo inak povedane, insighty, riesia ludia z mojho publika? Daj mi 10
> insightov z <akej?> komunity. + Ake problemy riesi tato komunita? Co ich trapi? Ake vtipne
> situacie pozna kazdy z tejto komunity?

Fill `<akej?>` with the community the audience belongs to (fitness people? marketers? what
unites them?). It returns insights you can immediately turn into posts.

## 3/7 How to brainstorm - don't; build an idea bank
Brainstorming is bullshit. Locking a room for an hour does not spawn a million great ideas,
because you can't evaluate them soberly - everyone thinks their own idea is best. You stop
struggling for ideas the moment you build your own **idea bank (napadovnik)**: a store of post
ideas where each idea gets a name and a linked reference, insight, or description. Collect ideas
constantly, anywhere (shower, bed), so keep the bank somewhere always on you - phone notes or a
task tool like Asana.

## 4/7 How to plan - whole months at a time
It's November, time to plan December (start on Nov 1).
- Look at your audience's topics and your idea bank.
- Pick ideas that fit the month's theme and drop them into the calendar.
- Lay out **12-30+ raw post drafts** for the month. 12 a month is enough to start.
- **Important note 1:** you are NOT creating posts yet, only dropping raw ideas into the
  calendar. That's all for today. Then close the laptop and come back the next day.
- **Important note 2:** when laying out content, do NOT plan selling content beyond **10-20%**
  max. There are better ways to sell on social than turning it into teleshopping.

### First versions (next day)
Next morning, turn the ideas into first versions - raw scripts. Write the script/story of each
post (e.g. a carousel script, or a short TikTok/reel/YouTube-short script; they're the same).
Goal: rough out roughly how each post should look. Note: do NOT write the caption (popis) yet -
that's simple and comes last (see Final tips). If writing the story is hard, a craft book on
writing (e.g. Stephen King's "On Writing") helps.

### Second versions (after a day's rest)
Never write second versions the same day; let first versions rest a day. Then:
- **Read the first version aloud** and ask honestly: would this post appeal to ME if it popped
  up in my feed? Most people lie to themselves here and say "sure." Be honest - if it's crap,
  redo it. If you keep lying or endlessly postponing a post, drop it back into the idea bank
  and pull it out in a month, a few months, or a year.
- **Add a strong hook** for the first seconds of video (use the headlines skill).
- Then fill 3 things:
  - **What do I want to achieve with this post?** Exactly ONE goal (comments? reach? sales?).
    Never 2-3-4 goals from one post.
  - **What should the audience do when they see it?** Exactly ONE action (share? comment? like?
    save?). Never more than one.
  - **Apply a virality technique:** copy the style of a post that is working right now.

## 5/7 Send it forward (client revision)
Sit with the client and present next month's posts in a short presentation.
- Agree the number of revisions up front. 2-3 turns is fine; charge for each extra and put it
  in the contract.
- Define WHEN the client gives the first revision (at planning? at first versions?).
- Set a timeline, e.g. "first versions for revision by the 10th of the previous month, final
  planned by day XYZ."

## 6/7 Production
Make the videos, prepare graphics, assemble everything into a pre-final version of the posts.
Runs on the timeline below.

## 7/7 Ready - measure and repeat
Measure how it performs objectively and on a schedule. Don't watch likes daily - review once a
month. Export posts monthly (Creator Studio) and sort them in a table by goal (interaction is a
goal, etc.). Boost the best ones into ads, but mainly save and recycle them. Simple path: Meta
Business -> Insights -> Content, then play with the filtering.

## Timeline cookbook (planning December, as an example)
- By the start of Q4 you know December's monthly theme (from the thematic plan). Prep starts in
  October with a discussion between the social media manager and the client about what to do
  thematically in December.
- By **Nov 1**: first versions of posts ready.
- By **end of week 1 of November**: second versions ready, plus graphics briefs and video
  scripts and everything needed.
- **Client revision must happen before production.**
- **Week 2:** production (graphics and posts).
- **Week 3:** content ready to process; deliver final versions to client (only small-detail
  feedback at this point).

## Final tips & tricks

### Writing captions (popis) - write them LAST
Don't write fluffy marketing copy. Give people exactly what they came for, immediately - don't
bury the payoff word 25 words deep in fluff. Cut filler. Be specific (say exactly what's
included). Write like you're explaining it to a friend of a friend.

Anti-pattern: the "teleshopping" voice (think the classic infomercial pitch - "Tired of your
old vacuum? We have a REVOLUTIONARY solution..."). If someone commented asking for a specific
free resource, don't open with "Do you want to truly captivate people online and create one
content gem after another?" - they don't want that, they want the thing they asked for. Give
it to them in the first line:

> Ahoj, tak ti posielam [to, co si pytal]. Najdes to, ked klikns na odkaz nizsie. [Jedna
> konkretna veta o tom, co je vnutri.] Odkaz nizsie.

### When to post
- **Location matters.** If your audience is in Germany, post via a German VPN (some scheduling
  tools, e.g. Buffer, let you set the posting location). A creator posting from Thailand saw
  lower reach because posts were shown to a Thai audience; back home it was fine.
- Post when YOU can interact (see routine below).

### Before / during / after posting: the interaction routine
If you activate your audience in the ~15 minutes before and after publishing, the post gets more
reach and better interaction. Quick routine:
- 15 min before publishing, reply to every DM in your inbox.
- Reply to all comments under your old posts.
- Ideally reply with questions (ask people "how" questions) so they reply back - this builds an
  interaction bubble around publish time.
- Then comment on other people's posts, with questions.
- So: schedule the post for a time when you can interact.

## How to use this skill
1. Run Part A: define audience (3 clients + descriptions) -> generate insights with the formula
   -> stock the idea bank.
2. Run Part B: drop raw ideas into the month's calendar (keep selling <=20%), then first
   versions (next day), then second versions (after rest) with hook + ONE goal + ONE action +
   virality copy.
3. Follow the timeline cookbook for client revision and production.
4. Write captions last using the "give them what they came for" rule; publish on the
   interaction routine.
5. Review monthly, recycle winners. Repeat.
