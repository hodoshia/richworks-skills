# LinkedIn Post Templates (thought-leadership / ghostwriting)

**Premise:** LinkedIn is not about quantity of posts. Every single piece must carry clear
value and an "aha moment". The easiest way to hit that: use proven structures. These 5
templates let even a total beginner write strong posts (copy-paste, fill, publish).

**Ghostwriting context:** posts get published through other people's personal profiles
(directors, managers, founders), but the texts are prepared by the social media manager based
on interviews, materials, and research. The content feels personal and authentic even though
someone else writes it. The advantage of ghostwriting is consistency and the ability to
communicate expert topics accessibly.

**Language:** produce output in the brand's language (default Slovak), in a plain, direct,
no-bullshit voice. Build authority through value, not by sounding perfect.

## The 5 templates

### 1. Mistakes & lessons
The fastest way to give value: show where you got burned and what you learned.
Structure: **Decision -> Result -> Mistake -> Lesson.**
Why it works: you build authority not by posing as flawless, but by showing your vulnerable
path to others.
Example: "The biggest mistake I made hiring? I thought more CVs = better choice. 300 CVs came
in, zero quality. Lost 2 weeks of my life. Lesson: fewer but qualified candidates = more value."

### 2. Frameworks
People love simple step-by-step methods. "Want to solve X? Do 1-2-3."
Structure: **Name the problem -> Give clear steps -> Show the result.**
Why it works: frameworks are sticky - easy to remember and to apply.
Example: "Want your LinkedIn posts to get 5x more reach? 1) Start with a story or shock. 2) Give
a practical tip. 3) End with a question that starts a discussion..."

### 3. The future ("here's why")
Business audiences love speculation; on social, even more so. Use it to your advantage, but stay
ethical. "Where is all this heading...?"
Structure: **Where the industry is today -> Where you think it's going -> Why (back it with
data) -> What it means for the reader.**
Why it works: you don't have to be right. Just showing a vision makes you look like a leader.
Example: "Within 3 years, 80% of B2B sales will happen without phone calls. Why? Gen Z rejects
cold calls. A Salesforce survey: 74% of young buyers prefer chat. If you're not building a
content funnel today, the competition will steamroll you in a few years."

### 4. Category view
The best authors don't just sell a brand - they educate about the whole category.
Structure: **What's wrong with the old category -> Name and explain the new category -> Show what
happens when people switch.**
Why it works: you move people from the "old world" to the "new world".
Example: "Campaigns are dead. Here's the new category: always-on content. Campaigns = short hype,
then silence. Always-on = builds the brand nonstop, lowers cost per reach, and raises trust."

### 5. Myths
People love insider info. This is your chance to reveal what's really going on.
Structure: **The common myth -> The reality -> What it changes for the reader.**
Example: "Myth: only boring expert articles work on LinkedIn. Reality: personal stories and
simple visuals get the most reach. When you start writing for people, not for colleagues, you
become an authority."

## Filling templates with AI
Once you have the structure, AI can massively speed up the work. Give your AI assistant a topic
and say "use template X, make me an outline...". Adjusted prompt (you MUST edit the parts in
[BRACKETS]):

```
Spravaj sa ako expert content strateg.

[Vyber si jednu z tychto sablon:
- Chyby & Poucenia
- Frameworky
- Predikcie buducnosti
- Kategoria
- Myty]

Tvoja uloha: pomoz mi spravit jednoduchy outline prispevku.

Spytaj sa ma len na to, co potrebujes:
- Nadpis alebo hlavnu temu
- Kontext (napr. co sa stalo, ak ide o chybu)
- Fakty alebo priklady, ktore chcem pouzit

Potom mi vrat prehladne body vo forme kratkych viet, ktore sa daju hned rozpracovat do postu.
```

Suddenly you have the skeleton in seconds. Add personal examples and you have a post that looks
like it came from a senior LinkedIn copywriter. The templates give you the skeleton; AI adds the
muscles.

## Two extra tricks when you don't know where to start
1. **Brain dump first.** Write 4-5 bullets about the topic you want to tackle, THEN feed it to
   the AI assistant - it tidies your thoughts.
2. **Helper prompt to expand creativity** (paste verbatim):

```
Daj mi 5 otazok, ktore si citatel polozi k tomuto bodu / teme: {SEM VLOZ BOD ALEBO TEMU, KTORU
CHCES RIESIT}.

Ku kazdej otazke priradi kratku hodnotnu odpoved (tip, priklad, cislo, insight).

Co bude citatel najpravdepodobnejsie chciet vediet, ked narazi na tento bod / temu?

A aku najhodnotnejsiu odpoved mu viem dat? (napr. tip, krok, poucenie, chyba, priklad, dovod,
prinos, statistika atd.)
```

Then go point by point, answer the questions, and add a short intro on top.

## The point
These templates pull you out of chaos and help you write content with a clear goal. If the
reader gets value -> they start to trust you -> they see you as an authority. Again and again.

## How to use this skill
1. Pick the template that fits the user's goal (vulnerability/authority -> mistakes; how-to ->
   frameworks; vision -> future; reframing the market -> category; contrarian -> myths).
2. Fill the structure with the user's real facts/examples (use the fill prompt for an outline).
3. If stuck, brain-dump 4-5 bullets or run the 5-questions helper prompt first.
4. Add a strong hook on top (see headlines) and end on one CTA / discussion question.
