# Profile Optimization (Instagram)

**Premise:** every day dozens to hundreds of people visit a profile and decide within
seconds whether to skip it or follow. Tune the profile so it passively convinces as many
of them as possible to hit Follow. Six sections decide this; get each to 100%.

**Language:** produce output in the brand's language (default Slovak). Keep a plain,
direct, no-bullshit voice.

## 1. Bio / profile description
Instagram shows 4 lines of the bio. Use each for a different purpose:
1. **Line 1:** what you do / how you help people.
2. **Line 2:** how you do it / who you can help.
3. **Line 3:** proof you do it well - show people they can trust you.
4. **Below the last line:** the URL link to your site - point to it.

Do NOT put in the bio:
- Hashtags (Instagram itself advises against this).
- Keywords already used in your username and name/title field - use different keywords
  here to grab attention.

## 2. Profile photo
It must be recognizably YOU within a second. Use a photo or graphic with your face, your
colors, logo, or other brand elements. A face or a company logo is the safe choice. Use
high resolution. Tip: use something loud and striking that stands out, because the photo
usually appears among other photos and stories. Flip it upside down if that helps it pop.

## 3. Username (@handle)
The first thing a prospect sees. Four rules:
- A short name is a good name.
- Max 2 words.
- No special characters (hard to type on mobile).
- No digits or other pointless characters.

Why: the name must be simple, memorable, and unique. If you really don't know what to use,
just use @namesurname or @surname.

## 4. Name / title field
The most underrated space on Instagram. Biggest mistake: copying your @handle into it.
Instagram's head Adam Mosseri stated this field should be used as the profile TITLE. Note
it also shows in the chat to anyone you DM. So put your name/brand first, then (with a
separator or without, depending on character count) add one or more keywords describing
what you do. Instagram uses this field in search: if someone searches a keyword like
"marketing", profiles that have that keyword in the name field surface.

## 5. Profile category
The most OVERrated section. Simpler than it looks: pick NO category. When people click a
category they can land on competitors who make similar content. Don't waste time
over-configuring social features. Settle this in a minute and move on.

## 6. Story highlights
Max 5 highlights are visible on screen; the first 5 matter most, and 5 is genuinely enough.
The most important ones:
1. Introduce yourself and your brand (ideally talking to camera).
2. Your free products - point to them with a sticker.
3. Your paid services and products.
4. Happy clients and their enthusiastic messages (social proof).
5. FAQ - frequently asked questions; you can send this highlight straight into chat to
   anyone who asks one of them.

You don't strictly need only 5: if you run a story series, highlights can also serve as its
individual episodes.

## How to use this skill
Walk the 6 sections in order and get each to 100%. A great profile is the base; pair it with
strategic content (see content-planning) and conversion tracking (see profile-growth).
