# Reels, TikTok & Video Strategy

**Key metric (legend):** VTR = watch-through rate, the % of your video the audience managed
to watch to the end. Most tactics here exist to raise VTR.

**Golden rule:** everything can be shortened and sped up. If you think it can't, try. Cram it
into the most dynamic 7-15 seconds possible - the platform rewards you with reach.

**Language:** produce scripts/output in the brand's language (default Slovak), in a plain,
direct voice.

## 7 points every viral reel shares
1. **Be dynamic.** Shoot reels as a montage of ~2-second clips, each from a different camera
   angle. Changing angles is the secret to better VTR.
2. **Don't know what to film?** Get inspired by foreign creators, YouTube, and trends. What
   worked once in one corner of the internet will work now in this corner too.
3. **Use captions/subtitles** when speaking to camera. They add dynamics (raise VTR) and
   ~50% of people watch without sound.
4. **Look into the camera, not at the screen.** The camera sits just above the screen; on
   video you can see where you look. Small change, huge difference.
5. **Use trending audio.** Find trending audio on TikTok's free source. What is trending on
   TikTok now will be trending on Instagram in ~2 weeks.
6. **Create interaction** in the hour you publish: post a story with an easy-answer sticker,
   reply to comments and DMs. Interaction triggers more interaction; if you create some, the
   platform rewards you with higher reach.
7. **Reverse the payoff.** Normally the twist (the biggest value) sits at the end of a video.
   TikTok flipped this. Put your biggest bomb at the START.

## Tactic: the reversed-payoff principle (second by second)
On the internet people are impatient. Deliver the value bomb first, then the filler that holds
attention. Map a video to seconds:
- **Seconds 1-2:** the biggest value. Something that grabs attention and holds it to the end.
  Use the curiosity effect - spark curiosity and keep it.
- **Seconds 3 to ~3/4 of the video:** hold curiosity by gradually revealing the twist.
- **At ~3/4:** now, just before the end, reveal everything, unpack it.
- **At the end:** never skip the call to action. Always tell the audience what you want
  (comment, like, web click). Ask for exactly ONE thing, never more at once.

## Strategy: the hero / hub / hygiene matrix
Originally a YouTube content model; works for Instagram and TikTok too.

- **Hero** - the main pillar. Videos you really invest in; advertising content released ~2-3
  times a year. Long-lived major campaigns, recyclable nearly forever; copy the brand promise
  and match the tone of voice 100%. Most impact (reach + interactions + media space) but
  expensive to produce.
- **Hub** - medium impact, harder to produce, posted less often than hygiene, medium lifespan.
  Tests, fun campaigns, how-to videos, anything that delivers value and costs more time and
  effort.
- **Hygiene** - classic "consumable" content: cheap, fast, low-energy, but ensures consistency
  and momentum. Mainly signals the channel is alive. The mandatory baseline (e.g. a basic
  instructional video).

**Example mapping for a personal/creator account:** carousels = hygiene (regularity,
consistency, dialogue with the audience); reels = hub (longer to make, more impact); stories +
DMs = hero, the main selling channel - after building the relationship in stories, you tune DM
tactics and sell services to people you have gotten close to.

Use this matrix to plan a whole video (or content) strategy.

## Exercise: 2 reel examples to copy

**List of options (short ~7s video):**
- Prepare a list of options (up to ~7).
- Start with a strong word/hook in the captions.
- Use trending audio and cut to it with a hand movement revealing each option.
- End with a call to action (e.g. follow).
- Make it very fast on purpose. If a person can't catch all options at once, they rewatch -
  which raises VTR.

**New-era meme:**
- Many meme creators say their memes have stopped working - so turn them into video.
- Use a funny clip; make a template like a meme post.
- Repeat it over and over. Few creators in a given local market do this yet, so there is open
  space.

**Your exercise:** find a competitor abroad (someone doing the same thing as you), analyze
their successful reels, write down the script they use, then apply that script to yourself and
your business.

## How to use this skill
1. For a single reel: build the script second-by-second with the reversed payoff, force it
   into 7-15s, plan captions + trending audio + angle changes, end on one CTA.
2. For strategy: classify the user's planned content into hero/hub/hygiene and balance it.
3. Always frame success around VTR and interaction, not vanity metrics.
