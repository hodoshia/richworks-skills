# Foundation Layer - Teaching AI Your Brand

Without context, AI gives you the same output as everyone else. The foundation layer
is a folder of text files you build once, point your AI assistant to, and it makes every output
yours - your voice, your audience, your positioning. The more complete it is, the
better the output. The more often you use AI without it, the more you waste time
editing generic results.

**Language:** default output in Slovak, plain and direct.

## The 6 files to build

### 1. Audience Delight Profile
What your customers love about you. Pull from real reviews, testimonials, DMs, comments.
Verbatim quotes are gold. Include:
- What they specifically praise (not what you think they should praise)
- Which parts of your product or service they mention most
- Emotional outcomes they describe ("I finally felt confident", "I stopped wasting hours")
- Words and phrases they use repeatedly

### 2. Audience ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)
A written profile of your ideal customer. Include:
- Who they are (age, role, life situation)
- What they want to achieve
- What they fear or hate
- When they typically come to you and why
- What they tried before finding you
- Objections they have before buying
- Their language - the words they use when describing their problem (not your marketing words)

### 3. Creator Style (Tone of Voice)
How YOU write and speak. Be specific, not vague ("warm but direct" is useless to AI).
Include:
- Words you use freely, including industry jargon or anglicisms you keep untranslated
- Words you never use (list them explicitly)
- Sentence length preference (short punchy fragments? long flowing paragraphs?)
- Things you always do (start with a hook? always end with a question?)
- Things you never do (no bullet walls? no corporate phrases? no passive voice?)
- 5-10 examples of your best posts or articles you are happy with - one set per
  platform if your style differs by channel (e.g. Slovak LinkedIn vs. blog vs. Instagram)
- Platform-specific rules (e.g. "on LinkedIn: always start with a one-line hook, then
  a line break, then body. Never use more than 3 bullet points.")

### 4. Customer Journey
How customers find you, decide, buy, and what happens after. Especially important
for online products. Include:
- How they typically discover you (referral, search, social, paid)
- What their decision process looks like (how long, what they compare)
- What makes them buy vs. not buy
- What happens in the first week after buying
- Common friction points or complaints

### 5. Market Positioning
Where you sit relative to competitors. Include:
- Who your main competitors are
- What they do well
- What you do better or differently
- What you deliberately do not do
- The one thing only you can say credibly

### 6. README (instructions for the AI)
A short file telling the AI exactly how to use the other files. Example:

```
This folder is my foundation layer. When you write, suggest strategies,
create content, analyze my audience, or give feedback on my work:
1. Read the ICP and Delight Profile first - know who you're writing for.
2. Use Creator Style - match my voice exactly. Never use words on the "never" list.
3. Reference Positioning when suggesting angles or differentiators.
4. If you learn something new about my audience or voice during our session,
   flag it and ask if you should update the relevant file.
```

## How to point your AI assistant to it

Once you have the folder, say this at the start of each project or session:

```
V priečinku [nazov priecinka] najdes moj foundation layer. Kedykolvek pre mna
nieco pises, navrhuj strategie, tvoris content alebo sa rozpravame o mojich
zakaznikoch - vzdy si tento priecinok precitaj najprv.
```

Or set it as a persistent instruction so you never have to say it again - in a
ChatGPT Project or Custom GPT, a Gemini Gem, a Claude Project, or whatever your tool
calls its custom-instructions / saved-context feature.

## How to build it fast

Do not sit down and write it from scratch - it takes hours and comes out generic.
Instead:
1. Voice-record yourself for 15-20 minutes answering these questions out loud: Who
   are my best customers? What do they love about me? How do I sound when explaining
   my work to a friend? Who am I NOT for? How am I different from competitors?
2. Drop the transcript into your AI assistant. Say: "Structure this into the 6 foundation layer
   files. Ask me what is missing."
3. The AI asks clarifying questions. Answer them. Done in under an hour.

Alternatively: collect 20-30 recent customer reviews or messages, drop them in, and
let the AI build the Delight Profile and ICP from real customer language.

## Keep it alive

A foundation layer that is 6 months old stops working. Review it quarterly, or
whenever something changes: new audience segment, new product, new competitors,
changed tone. The best habit: when the AI learns something new in a session (a
customer insight, a new objection, a phrasing that resonated), say "add this to
my foundation layer."

## Without it vs. with it

Without: paste a brief, get a result that sounds like a marketing textbook.
With: paste the same brief, get something that sounds like you wrote it on your best day.

The difference is not the AI model. The difference is the context you give it.

## How to use this skill

1. Build the 6 files (voice-record method is fastest for getting started).
2. Point your AI assistant to the folder at the start of each project.
3. Update it when you learn something new about your audience or voice.
4. Add platform-specific Creator Style files as you expand to new channels.
